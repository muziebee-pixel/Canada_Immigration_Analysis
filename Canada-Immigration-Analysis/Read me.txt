# 🇨🇦 Canada Immigration Analysis — The Japa Story In Data

## 📊 Project Overview
This project analyses Canadian Permanent Residence immigration 
trends from 2015 to 2025 using real official data from the 
Canadian government (IRCC).

The analysis focuses on:
- Nigeria's journey to becoming the 4th largest source country 
  for Canadian PR globally
- Which immigration pathways work best
- Which provinces receive the most immigrants
- The COVID-19 impact on immigration and Nigeria's recovery

---

## 🎯 Key Findings

### Finding 1 — Nigeria Is #4 Globally
Nigeria ranked 4th out of 218 countries in Canadian PR 
admissions in 2024 with 20,395 admissions — and #1 among 
ALL African countries.

### Finding 2 — Economic Pathway Dominates
Over 58% of all Canadian PR admissions come through the 
Economic pathway (Express Entry + PNP). This is the fastest 
and most reliable route to Canadian PR.

### Finding 3 — Ontario Leads But Has Competition
Ontario receives over 40% of all immigrants. However Manitoba 
and Saskatchewan PNP programs offer better entry chances for 
applicants with lower Express Entry scores.

### Finding 4 — COVID Impact and Recovery
COVID-19 caused Nigeria's admissions to crash from 12,600 in 
2019 to 6,360 in 2020. By 2025 Nigeria had recovered to 
16,355 — a 157% growth from the COVID low point.

### Finding 5 — Nigeria Leads Africa
Nigeria's PR admissions are almost double the second highest 
African country (Eritrea) — demonstrating Nigerian immigrants' 
strong educational qualifications and English language 
proficiency advantage in the Canadian Express Entry system.

## 🔄 Project Process

### Step 1 — Data Collection
Downloaded official IRCC datasets from open.canada.ca covering
PR admissions by country and by province/immigration category.

### Step 2 — Data Cleaning (Excel + Power Query)
- Replaced "--" values with 0 (IRCC privacy protection)
- Removed commas from numbers
- Fixed messy merged header rows
- Converted wide format to long format using Unpivot
- Removed grand total rows
- Created 4 clean datasets ready for analysis

### Step 3 — SQL Analysis (MySQL Workbench)
- Imported 4 CSV files into MySQL database
- Wrote 10 analytical queries covering:
  - Nigeria yearly trend
  - Top 10 source countries
  - Africa comparison
  - COVID impact analysis
  - Pathway popularity ranking
  - Province rankings
  - Province vs pathway breakdown

### Step 4 — Dashboard (Power BI)
- Connected cleaned CSV files to Power BI
- Created DAX measures for KPIs
- Built 2 dashboard pages:
  - Page 1: Executive Overview (global picture)
  - Page 2: Nigeria Deep Analysis

## 💡 Recommendations

Based on the data analysis:

**For individuals planning to immigrate to Canada:**
1. Focus on Express Entry — it accounts for the majority of 
   Economic pathway admissions
2. If your CRS score is low — target Manitoba or Saskatchewan 
   PNP programs which have lower score requirements
3. Atlantic provinces (Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, PEI, 
   Newfoundland) offer special Atlantic Immigration Programs 
   with more accessible entry requirements
4. Avoid targeting only Ontario — competition is highest there

**For policy makers and immigration consultants:**
1. Nigeria shows sustained high demand for Canadian PR — 
   dedicated Nigeria-focused immigration information programs 
   would serve this growing market
2. COVID demonstrated the need for digital processing systems 
   that can maintain operations during global disruptions
3. The 2023-2024 dip in admissions reflects Canada's 
   immigration cap policy — numbers are expected to stabilize
